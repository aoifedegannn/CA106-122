About Gymnastics

The term gymnastics originates from the Greek meaning "to exercise naked". It was originally created by Doctor Friedrich Ludwig Jahn, a german man known as the father of modern gymnastics, who developed exercises for men. It was he who introduced the vaulting horse, parallel bar, horizontal bar, balance beam, ladder and pommel horse. Gymnastics is a sport that requires balance, strength, agility and flexibility. It involves every muscle group along with coordination and endurance. The most commonly practised form of gymnastics is artist gymnastics.

For women this consists of:

  • Beam
  • Uneven Bars
  • the Events Floor
  • Vault

For men this consists of:

  • Beam
  • Uneven Bars
  • Parellel Bars
  • Horizontal Bar
  • the Events Floor
  • Vault
  • Rings
  • Pommel Horse

The governing body for gymnastics is the FIG (Fédération Internationale de Gymnastique) and it covers eight sports, including:

  1. Gymnastics for All
  2. Men's and Women's Artistic Gymnastics
  3. Rythmitic Gymnastics
  4. Trampoline
  5. Aerobics
  6. Acrobatics
  7. Tumbling
  8. Parkour
